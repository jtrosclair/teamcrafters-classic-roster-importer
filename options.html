Import a roster from a CSV

1. Start from the sample

The sample is a complete, valid 85-player roster with every column filled in. Open it in Excel or Google Sheets, change the players to whatever you want, and save it as CSV.

2. Upload your edited CSV

Every rating must be filled in with a number from 0–99. Don't include OVR — it's always calculated from the player's ratings and position, so a supplied value is rejected. Bio fields (height, weight, class, skin tone…) can be left blank — those fall back to the base template's values.

Roster requirements

Your roster has to be able to field a team, so the import checks:

  • 45–85 players total
  • Every rating 0–99, no blanks (don't include OVR — it's calculated)
  • Minimum at each position:

QB2LT1LOLB1
HB3LG1MLB2

Two units also have a combined minimum on top of those:

  • 8 offensive linemen across LT, LG, C, RG, RT
  • 3 outside linebackers across LOLB and ROLB

How you distribute them is up to you — three backup tackles and no backup guard is fine — as long as no single spot is left empty.

If anything's off, the importer tells you the exact row and column — nothing is imported until it all passes.

Players are matched to roster slots by position, best overall rating first. If you have more players at a position than the template does, the extras fill open spots elsewhere; if you have fewer, the leftover template players are removed.
